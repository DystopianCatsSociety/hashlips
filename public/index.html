FAQs




¿What are NFTs and how do they work?
NFTs is an acronym for Non-Fungible Tokens. Non-fungible in the sense that they are unique and cannot be traded as fungible assets like Bitcoin. Take, for example, if you trade one Bitcoin to get another, nothing changes, you still get the same Bitcoin; it is the opposite in the case of NFT. Each NFT has a distinct signature that is used to designate and verify ownership of assets; there are not two original copies. NFTs are digital assets that are often representations of real-world objects, such as game items, videos, music, collectibles, virtual assets, and art..

Why does NFT art have value?
Just like physical art, four factors contribute to the value of an NFT art: how expensive it gets. The first is "market-driven value," which depends on the speculated resale value and popularity of the artist. The second is the “subjective value”. It is about the moral statement or the political message that art conveys: how it makes you feel..

What are NFTs used for?
NFTs can be used to indicate ownership of rare assets which represents real-world items like real estate and artwork. It’s important to note however that purchasing an NFT that includes the copyright of the original artwork does not mean its copyright is automatically transferred to you. While you can sell the NFT itself, you are prohibited from reproducing or selling its content.
